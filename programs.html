Our Programs

On this page, you will be able to see our all of our current and future educational programs and initiatives.

Current Projects

Our current projects include helping middle and high schools set up and operate entrepreneurship programs at their institutions. We offer curriculum development and teacher training services as well as consultations and in-class instruction support in order to ensure the success of our schools' initiatives.

In addition to offering services to help middle and high schools set up Entrepreneurship programs at their institutions, we also offer Entrepreneurship workshops and programs directly to students.

Entrepreneurship Pilot Course at Ann Arbor Open School

Brightfruit helped Ann Arbor Open School set up and operate their first Entrepreneurship elective course. Brightfruit provided Ann Arbor Open School with the information needed to get the elective course approved by the district, as well as the curriculum, detailed lesson plans, project details, instructor mentoring, consultation, and in-class instruction support. Brightfruit also assisted in scheduling guest speakers such as local entrepreneur Sandy Ryder of Wild Swan Theatre Company, and in reserving a space for students to sell their final product at a nearby local foods grocery store and cafe Argus Farm Stop.

19 students in the 8th grade Brightfruit pilot class at Ann Arbor Open School made products such as bath bombs, chocolate-covered pretzels, collage bookmarks, and cat toys. They made and sold these products as they practiced the iterative model for product and business development.

Summer Entrepreneurship Program in Downtown Ann Arbor

Brightfruit is offering a 3-week Entrepreneurship Summer Program where students meet three times a week for 3 hours for workshops aimed to develop entrepreneurial skill sets, while working in teams on their own student project. Learn the iterative framework for product and business development, how to conduct customer and problem discovery, how to develop effective solutions, and launch your own business. All in 3 weeks!

Future Projects

Our future projects are plans we have for expansion. We are currently in conversation with Ann Arbor Public School District about expanding our in-school and after-school services to other schools in the Ann Arbor area such as Forsythe Middle School, Community High School, Pioneer High School, and Skyline High School. We are also in the process of discussing a potential partnership with Clonlara Schools to help develop a digitla entrepreneurship curriculum that will be integrated into their network of distance-learning and homeschooled students, as well as mentors.

We are excited about our work, and we hope that you are too. We are always looking for new partners to help us expand our services and reach more students. Expanding In-School and After-School Programs

Brightfruit is looking to expand its services to other middle and high schools. Our goal is to help these institutions successfully set up and maintain Entrepreneurship programs at their institutions. We are also looking to offer after-school programs for middle and high school students to learn about Entrepreneurship and develop their skills while working on real projects.

Digital Entrepreneurship Curriculum Development for Distance-Learning and Home-School Students

Brightfruit is currently in conversation with Clonlara Schools to form a partnership for the development of an Entrepreneurship curriculum that will be integrated to Clonlara School's online distance-learning and home-schooling curriculum.

Brightfruit sees an opportunity to integrate our Entrepreneurship curriculum with the existing online distance-learning and home-schooling infrastructures by providing training for home-school mentors as well as project guides for students who the mentors work closely with.
