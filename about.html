What is Brightfruit?

Brightfruit is a team of students and educators from the University of Michigan and the surrounding Ann Arbor area, working to bring experiential Entrepreneurship programs to middle and high school students. Our curriculum was developed by entrepreneurs, faculty and students of the University of Michigan, and local Ann Arbor public and private school teachers. This collaboration has yielded an Entrepreneurship curriculum that provides an introduction to Entrepreneurship through actually engaging in Entrepreneurship, and develops essential technical and interpersonal skills such as familiarity with the iterative framework for product and business development, customer and problem discovery, solution development, and service leadership.

We currently provide services that help middle and high schools set up and operate an Entrepreneurship program at their academic institutions. Our services range from curriculum development to teacher and mentor training, guest speaker scheduling, consultation sessions, and in-class instruction support.

Our Team

Navanas Chetsandtikhun

Founder and Lead Educator

Navanas has over two years of teaching the Entrepreneurship Practicum course at the University of Michigan, achieving a total of over $230,000 in revenue from student class projects. He has over three years of experience in socially-engaged design teams in both leadership and design capacities. He has also been involved with many student entrepreneurship organizations including being a fellow of the optiMize Social Innovation Challenge and the Barger Leadership Institute, as well as participating in Innovation in Action and Learning Levers Programs. He is the lead educator and curriculum developer of Brightfruit.

Madison K. Flood

Lead Researcher

Madison has extensive research experience in human-computer interaction at the University of Michigan and her anthropology background allows for a deep understanding of different cultures and stakeholder interactions from a multitude of socioeconomic and cultural lenses. She is involved in the needs assessment and research portion of our project, making sure that our curriculum design is supported by research, and developing a framework to measure the effectiveness of our program. She is also leading the development of Brightfruit's digital curriculum for homeschooled and distance-learning students.
